Valuation Core
Perpetuity (No Growth)
Value = Cash Flow / Discount Rate
Use when cash flow is constant forever. e.g. $100 / 10% = $1,000.
Gordon Growth Model
Value = CF / (r − g)
Requires g < r. e.g. $100 / (10% − 5%) = $2,000. Also used for DCF Terminal Value.
Enterprise Value
TEV = Equity Value + Debt + Preferred + Minority Interest − Cash
Represents total cost to acquire the business. Capital-structure neutral.
Equity Value (Market Cap)
Equity Value = Share Price × Diluted Shares
Value to common shareholders only. Always use diluted share count.
Treasury Stock Method (Options)
Net New Shares = Options − (Proceeds / Share Price)
Proceeds = Options × Strike Price. Only applies when in-the-money (Strike < Price).
EV / EBITDA Multiple
Multiple = TEV / EBITDA
Most common EV-based multiple. Pair EV with metrics that don't deduct interest (EBIT, Revenue, EBITDA).
DCF & Discount Rate
Unlevered Free Cash Flow (UFCF)
UFCF = EBIT × (1 − t) + D&A − CapEx − ΔNWC
Also called FCFF. "Unlevered" = before interest. Discounted at WACC.
Terminal Value — Gordon Growth
TV = FCFn × (1 + g) / (WACC − g)
Long-term growth rate ≈ nominal GDP (1.5–3%). TV typically = 60–80% of total DCF value.
Terminal Value — Exit Multiple
TV = EBITDAn × Exit Multiple
Exit multiple based on current comparable company multiples. Cross-check with Gordon Growth.
WACC
WACC = (E/V) × Ke + (D/V) × Kd × (1 − t)
V = E + D. Use market value of Equity; book value of Debt. Add Preferred if applicable.
Cost of Equity — CAPM
Ke = Rf + β × ERP
Rf = 10-yr govt bond. ERP ≈ 5–7%. β reflects both business risk and leverage risk.
Unlever Beta
βu = βL / (1 + D/E × (1 − t))
Removes effect of leverage to isolate pure business risk. Always βu ≤ βL. Relever using target's D/E.
Accounting & Financial Metrics
EBITDA
EBITDA = Operating Income + D&A
Add back D&A from Cash Flow Statement (often buried in COGS/SG&A on the Income Statement).
Free Cash Flow (Simple)
FCF = Cash Flow from Operations − CapEx
Represents discretionary cash flow after funding the business. Positive & growing = self-sufficient.
Working Capital
NWC = Operating Current Assets − Operating Current Liabilities
Exclude Cash, Investments, and Debt. Increase in NWC = use of cash (negative on CFS).
ROIC
ROIC = NOPAT / Invested Capital
NOPAT = Operating Income × (1−t). Invested Capital = Equity + Debt + Preferred − Cash.
Net Interest Margin (Banks)
NIM = (Interest Income − Interest Expense) / Avg Earning Assets
Core bank profitability metric. Rising rates usually expand NIM if loans reprice faster than deposits.
Net Operating Loss (DTA)
DTA from NOL ≈ Tax Rate × NOL Balance
NOLs can offset up to 80% of future taxable income (U.S. post-TCJA). Key in M&A analysis.
LBO & Returns
Equity Proceeds (LBO Exit)
Equity Proceeds = Exit EV − Remaining Debt
Exit EV = Exit Year EBITDA × Exit Multiple. Remaining Debt = Initial Debt − Debt Repaid.
Money-on-Money Multiple (MoM)
MoM = Equity Proceeds / Initial Equity
Initial Equity = Purchase EV × Equity %. Simple return measure, ignores time value.
IRR Rules of Thumb (5 years)
2x ≈ 15% · 2.5x ≈ 20% · 3x ≈ 25%
Same MoM over fewer years = higher IRR. 2x in 3 yrs ≈ 26%. 3x in 3 yrs ≈ 44%.
LBO FCF (for Debt Repayment)
FCF = EBITDA − Net Interest − Cash Taxes ± ΔNWC − CapEx
Cash available for debt repayment = Beginning Cash + FCF − Min Cash − Mandatory Repayments.
Debt Capacity (LevFin)
Max Debt = EBITDA × Max Debt/EBITDA
Typical LBO leverage: 4–6× EBITDA. Also check EBITDA/Interest ≥ 2–2.5× (coverage ratio).
Dividend Recap Impact on IRR
Earlier cash out → ↑ IRR, same MoM
PE firm borrows more, pays itself a dividend. Boosts IRR by returning capital early without a full exit.
Merger Models (M&A)
Accretion / Dilution Test
Seller Yield > Weighted Cost → Accretive
Seller Yield = Seller NI / Purchase Equity Value. Weighted Cost = blend of cash, debt, stock costs.
Cost of Stock Funding
Cost of Stock = 1 / Buyer P/E
If Buyer P/E > Seller purchase P/E in a 100% stock deal → accretive.
After-Tax Cost of Debt / Cash
After-Tax Cost = Rate × (1 − t)
Interest is tax-deductible. At 25% tax, a 10% debt rate costs only 7.5% after tax.
Goodwill (Acquisition)
Goodwill = Purchase Price − Fair Value of Net Assets
Net Assets = Assets − Liabilities at fair value. First allocate to identifiable intangibles, then Goodwill plugs the gap.
Shares Issued in Stock Deal
New Shares = Purchase Equity Value / Buyer Share Price
Combined EPS = Combined NI / (Buyer Shares + New Shares). Compare to standalone Buyer EPS.
Purchase Premium
Premium = (Offer Price − Pre-Announcement Price) / Pre-Announcement Price
Typical control premiums: 20–30%+. Precedent transactions include this; public comps do not.
Real Estate & REITs
Net Operating Income (NOI)
NOI = Property Revenue − Operating Expenses − Property Taxes
Excludes interest, debt repayment, and most CapEx. Real estate equivalent of EBITDA.
Cap Rate
Cap Rate = Forward NOI / Property Value
Reciprocal of a valuation multiple. 5% Cap Rate = 20× NOI. Lower Cap Rate → higher value.
FFO (Funds From Operations)
FFO = Net Income + RE D&A − Gains on Sales
Real estate D&A added back because properties typically appreciate. Replaces EPS for REITs.
AFFO (Adjusted FFO)
AFFO = FFO − Maintenance CapEx − Straight-Line Rent Adj.
More conservative than FFO. Closer to true distributable cash flow. Use P/AFFO for valuation.
Key Ratios to Memorise
Leverage Ratio
Debt / EBITDA
Investment grade: <3×. LBO range: 4–6×. Higher = riskier. Lenders set max covenant.
Interest Coverage
EBITDA / Interest Expense
Healthy: ≥2.5×. Below 1× = company can't cover interest from operations.
Price / Earnings (P/E)
P/E = Equity Value / Net Income
Equity Value multiple — Net Income already deducts interest, so pair with Equity, not EV.
P/Book Value (Banks)
P/BV = Equity Value / Book Value of Equity
P/TBV (Tangible Book) is most common in bank M&A. Excludes Goodwill and intangibles.
LTV / CAC (SaaS)
LTV / CAC > 3× = Healthy
LTV = lifetime customer value. CAC = customer acquisition cost. Net Revenue Retention > 100% = expansion exceeds churn.
Utility Allowed Revenue
Revenue = Pre-Tax Income + Interest + O&M + D&A
Pre-Tax Income = Allowed NI / (1−t). Allowed NI = Equity Base × Authorized ROE.
